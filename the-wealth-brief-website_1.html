Actually true

Six things about credit most people never get told plainly.

01 · PAYMENT HISTORY
It's the single biggest factor.
Payment history makes up roughly 35% of a FICO score — more than any other category. One late payment can matter more than several other factors combined.
02 · UTILIZATION
Under 30% helps. Under 10% helps more.
Utilization is how much of your available credit you're using. Even if you pay in full every month, a high balance on statement day can still lower your score.
03 · OLD ACCOUNTS
Closing your oldest card can backfire.
Length of credit history matters. Closing your longest-standing account can shorten your average account age and lower your available credit at the same time.
04 · HARD INQUIRIES
One application isn't a big deal.
A single hard inquiry typically costs a handful of points and fades from impact within about a year — it's rarely the reason a score drops significantly.
05 · INCOME
Your income isn't part of the score.
Credit scores measure how you manage credit, not how much you earn. Two people with identical habits and different incomes can have the same score.
06 · CHECKING YOUR OWN SCORE
Looking at your own score never hurts it.
Checking your own credit is a "soft" inquiry and has zero impact on your score — there's no reason to avoid looking.
